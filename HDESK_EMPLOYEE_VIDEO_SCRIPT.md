## SCENE 12: UNDERSTANDING SLA (SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENTS)
**[Duration: 1 minute 15 seconds]**

**[Screen: SLA dashboard showing priority levels]**

**Narrator:**
"Let's talk about Service Level Agreements - your guarantee of timely support.

**What is SLA?**

SLA defines how quickly you can expect a response and resolution based on your ticket's priority level.

**Our SLA Commitments:**

**HIGH Priority (Red)**
- First Response Time: 30 minutes
- Resolution Time: 1 business day
- Examples: System down, can't access critical applications, major hardware failure

**MEDIUM Priority (Yellow)**  
- First Response Time: 4 hours
- Resolution Time: 2-3 business days
- Examples: Software glitches, minor connectivity issues, non-critical requests

**LOW Priority (Green)**
- First Response Time: 1 business day
- Resolution Time: 3-5 business days
- Examples: General questions, enhancement requests, minor issues with workarounds

**Business Hours:**
Our SLA clock runs during business hours:
- Monday through Thursday: 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM
- Friday: 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM

**Special Rule for Fridays:**
Tickets submitted after 4:00 PM on Fridays automatically pause until Monday morning at 8:00 AM. This ensures fair SLA tracking.

**Weekend and After-Hours:**
The SLA clock pauses during:
- Weekends (Saturday and Sunday)
- After 4:00 PM on weekdays
- Before 8:00 AM on weekdays

While you can submit tickets anytime, the response clock follows business hours.

**SLA Status Indicators:**
In your ticket details, you'll see:
- Green indicator: Well within SLA
- Yellow indicator: At risk of breaching SLA  
- Red indicator: SLA breached - escalated to management"

## VIDEO PRODUCTION NOTES FOR HEYGEN AI

### Visual Style
- Clean, professional interface demonstrations
- Use smooth screen transitions
- Highlight buttons and fields with animated arrows or circles
- Use zoom effects for important details
- Include subtle background music (upbeat, professional)

### Avatar Selection
- Choose a professional, friendly avatar
- Consider having IT/HR representative appearance
- Warm, approachable tone
- Professional business attire

### Pacing
- Speak at moderate pace (not too fast)
- Pause 2-3 seconds between major sections
- Allow time for viewers to see screen actions
- Include "Let's take a closer look" transitions

### Screen Recording Tips
- Use demo account with sample data
- Prepare ticket examples beforehand
- Show smooth mouse movements
- Use actual ticket numbers and realistic scenarios
- Include both desktop and mobile views

### Accessibility
- Add closed captions
- Use high-contrast visuals
- Clear, large text on screen
- Avoid fast animations
