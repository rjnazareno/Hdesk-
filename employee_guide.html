System Overview

Hdesk is your company's IT Help Desk system. It lets you submit support requests (tickets) to the IT and HR departments, track their progress, and communicate directly with the staff handling your issue.

What You Can Do

Submit Requests

Create support tickets for IT or HR issues with detailed descriptions and file attachments.

Track Progress

Monitor the status of all your tickets from submission to resolution in real time.

Communicate

Send messages and replies to the IT/HR staff working on your ticket.

Get Notified

Receive notifications when your ticket status changes or staff replies.

Manage Profile

Update your contact info, profile picture, and password at any time.

Attach Files

Upload screenshots, documents, or other files to help describe your issue.

Navigation

After logging in, you'll see a sidebar on the left with these main sections:

Home (Dashboard) My Requests Create Ticket

On mobile devices, tap the menu button in the top-left corner to open the sidebar.

2

Logging In

Access Hdesk through your web browser. You'll use the username and password provided by your IT department.

1

Open the Hdesk login page

Go to the Hdesk URL provided by your IT team in your web browser (e.g., Chrome, Edge, Firefox).

2

Enter your credentials

Type your Username (or email) and Password in the login form.

3

Click "Sign In"

You'll be redirected to your Employee Dashboard. If the credentials are incorrect, an error message will appear.

Tip: Check the "Remember me" option if you're on your personal work computer. Your session will still expire after 30 minutes of inactivity for security.

Creating a Support Request

When you need help from IT or HR, submit a support request (ticket). The form has three easy steps.

1
Department
2
Details
3
Review
1

Step 1 — Select Department

Choose which department your request is for:

IT Department
Hardware, software, network, access issues
HR Department
Documents, payroll, timekeeping, inquiries

Click a department card to proceed to the next step.

2

Step 2 — Fill in the Details

You'll be asked to fill in:

Category Select the type of issue (e.g., "Hardware", "Payroll Inquiry").
Subcategory Narrow down to a specific issue (e.g., "Laptop", "Salary Discrepancy"). If none fit, select "Other".
Specific Issue If available, pick the exact issue from a third dropdown.
Description Explain the problem in detail. The more info you provide, the faster IT/HR can help.
Priority Choose Low, Medium, or High. A suggested priority is auto-selected based on your category.
Attachment Upload a screenshot or document if it helps explain the issue (max 5 MB).

Tip: The ticket title is auto-generated from your category selections. You don't need to type one!

3

Step 3 — Review & Submit

Before submitting, you'll see a summary of everything you entered:

  • Department and category selections
  • Auto-generated ticket title
  • Your description
  • Priority level and SLA targets
  • Attached file (if any)

If everything looks good, click "Submit Request". You'll receive a confirmation with your unique ticket number (e.g., TKT-202603061).

Ticket Details & Replies

When you click on a ticket, you'll see its full details and a conversation thread with the assigned staff member.

Ticket Information

  • Ticket number
  • Title & description
  • Category & department
  • Priority level
  • Current status
  • Assigned IT/HR staff
  • Attachments (if any)
  • Created date & last update

Conversation Thread

  • All messages between you and the assigned staff
  • Reply box to send new messages
  • Staff messages labeled with a badge
  • Activity timeline of all status changes
  • Resolution notes (when resolved)

How to reply: Scroll down to the reply box at the bottom of the conversation thread, type your message, and click "Send Reply". The assigned staff member will be notified.

Notifications

Hdesk notifies you whenever something happens with your tickets. Look for the bell icon in the top-right corner of every page.

Notification Types

Ticket Created

Confirmation that your request was submitted.

Status Changed

Your ticket's status was updated by staff.

New Reply

IT/HR staff replied to your ticket.

Ticket Closed

Your ticket has been resolved and closed.

Managing Notifications

1

View all notifications

Click the bell icon to go to the Notifications page. Unread ones have a blue dot.

2

Mark as read

Click the checkmark on any notification, or use "Mark All as Read".

3

Delete a notification

Click the trash icon to remove a notification you no longer need.

The red badge on the bell icon shows how many unread notifications you have. It updates automatically.

Managing Your Profile

Access your profile from the user dropdown menu in the top-right corner (click your name or avatar, then select "Profile").

1

Update your profile picture

Click "Change Picture" to upload a new photo. Accepts JPG, PNG, or GIF (max 2 MB). Your initials are shown if no picture is set.

2

Edit contact information

You can update your personal email and contact number. Your official email and position are read-only (managed by admin).

3

Save your changes

Click "Update Profile" to save. You'll see a confirmation message once your changes are saved.

Changing Your Password

You can change your password from the Profile page (scroll down to the "Change Password" section).

1

Enter your current password

For security, you must verify your identity by entering your existing password first.

2

Enter your new password

Type a new password (minimum 6 characters). Use the eye icon to toggle visibility.

3

Confirm new password

Re-type the new password to make sure there are no typos. Both fields must match.

4

Click "Change Password"

A success message confirms the update. Use your new password the next time you log in.

10

Forgot Password

If you can't remember your password, use the self-service reset feature from the login page.

1

Click "Forgot password?"

On the login page, click the "Forgot password?" link below the sign-in button.

2

Enter your email address

Type the email address associated with your Hdesk account and click submit.

3

Check your email

You'll receive an email with a password reset link. The link is valid for 24 hours.

4

Set a new password

Click the link in the email, enter your new password twice, and submit. You can now log in with your new password.

Note: If you don't receive the email within a few minutes, check your spam/junk folder. If the issue persists, contact your IT department directly.

Understanding Ticket Statuses

Every ticket goes through a lifecycle. Here's what each status means:

Pending
In Progress
Resolved
Closed

Pending

Your ticket has been submitted and is waiting to be reviewed and assigned to a staff member.

In Progress

Your issue is actively being worked on. You may receive messages from staff asking for more details.

Resolved

The staff member has resolved your issue. You can view resolution notes in the ticket details.

Closed

The ticket is finalized and no further action is needed. You can still view it for your records.

Priority Levels

Low

Minor issues that don't prevent you from working. Handled during normal business flow.

Medium

Issues affecting your productivity but with a workaround available. Addressed with moderate urgency.

High

Critical issues preventing you from working entirely. Addressed as quickly as possible.

Tips & Best Practices

Do

  • Be descriptive — include error messages, what you were doing when the issue occurred, and screenshots when possible.
  • Choose the right category — this helps route your ticket to the right team faster.
  • Check your notifications — staff may ask follow-up questions. Responding quickly helps resolve your issue sooner.
  • One issue per ticket — if you have multiple problems, create separate tickets for each.
  • Keep your profile updated — make sure your email and phone number are current so staff can reach you.

Don't

  • Don't submit duplicate tickets — check "My Requests" first to see if you already have an open ticket for the same issue.
  • Don't set everything to "High" priority — reserve High for genuinely critical, work-blocking issues.
  • Don't share your login — your account is personal. Never share your username or password with colleagues.
  • Don't leave vague descriptions — "It's not working" doesn't help. Describe what isn't working and what you expected.
